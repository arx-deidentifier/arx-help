Re-identification risks

This view displays an overview of several measures for re-identification risks implemented by ARX. In the upper area of this perspective risk estimates are provided for three different attacker models. The prosecutor scenario, the journalist scenario and the marketer scenario. In the prosecutor model it is assumed that the attacker already knows that data about an individual is contained in the data set. In the journalist model, such background knowledge is not assumed. In the marketer model, it is assumed that the attacker is not interested in a specific individual but that she aims at re-identifying a larger number of individuals.

Thresholds can be provided for the highest risk of any record, the records that have a risk higher than this threshold and for the average proportion of successfully re-identified records.

More details about the methods implemented by ARX can be found in the book Guide to the De-Identification of Personal Health Information by Khaled el Emam.

In the lower part of the screen, measures are displayed which are based on the sample itself and a selected statistical model for population uniqueness:

  • Lowest prosecutor re-identification risk.
  • Individuals affected by lowest risk.
  • Highest prosecutor re-identification risk.
  • Individuals affected by highest risk.
  • Average prosecutor re-identification risk.
  • Fraction of unique records.
